# Packlinx — Company Context

## When to invoke this skill

Read this skill whenever you are:

* Making a strategic proposal of any kind
* Designing UX, copy, or content for users
* Prioritizing tickets or features
* Evaluating trade-offs between speed, cost, and quality
* Onboarding a new role or task you have not handled before
* Unsure whether a piece of work matters

If your decision touches users, vendors, or the founder's runway, you
should have this context loaded.

## Identity

* **Company:** Packlinx
* **Domain:** packlinx.com
* **Type:** B2B vertical directory and matching platform
* **Market:** South Korea, domestic
* **Industry:** Packaging and packing supply chain

## Mission

Solve the information asymmetry in Korea's packaging industry.
Consolidate every vendor involved in the packaging and packing supply
chain — manufacturers, packing services, printing, finishing, materials
suppliers — into a single searchable platform, so buyers can find
qualified suppliers without cold-calling, trade shows, or
word-of-mouth.

## What Packlinx does

Packlinx aggregates vendors across the entire packaging supply chain:

* Packaging manufacturers — all forms and materials
* Packing services — contract, fulfillment, and specialized operations
* Post-processing services — printing, finishing, value-add operations
* Materials suppliers — raw materials, components, and packaging inputs

Vendors are organized by every attribute relevant to a B2B buyer's
decision — capabilities, certifications, region, history, and any
factor that helps buyers identify qualified candidates. Buyers filter
and discover qualified candidates in one place.

## Users

**Primary users (buyers):** Korean businesses that need packaging.

* Across all business types — from product brands and manufacturers to
  retailers and service providers
* Across all industries — wherever a physical product needs packaging
* Across all roles — anyone responsible for sourcing packaging,
  regardless of company size or job title

**Secondary users (vendors):** Packaging companies seeking
discoverability.

* Often traditional, relationship-driven, low digital maturity
* Many do not have modern websites or marketing presence

## Operating constraints

* **Solo-founded.** One human operator (the board/founder). All
  execution runs through AI agents. Every system you propose must be
  sustainable by one human + agents. Three-FTE-equivalent processes do
  not exist for us.
* **Early stage.** Pre-revenue or early-revenue. Capital is limited.
  Token costs are covered by subscription, so the scarce resources are
  the founder's attention and the calendar — not API spend. Optimize
  for learning per unit of founder time and per week elapsed.
* **Korean-domestic.** All UX, copy, and content default to Korean
  language and Korean buyer expectations. Internal documents are in
  English for LLM clarity.
* **Two-sided market.** Vendors first, then buyers. An empty directory
  is worse than no directory. Vendor density in target categories is
  the early-stage moat.

## Strategic priorities (re-read on every heartbeat)

1. **Vendor coverage in priority categories.** Without vendors, buyer
   experience does not exist.
2. **Trust signals.** B2B buyers de-risk before they commit. Any
   signal that helps a buyer assess vendor credibility, capability,
   and reliability matters more than visual polish.
3. **Time-to-find.** The single buyer outcome that matters: the speed
   at which a buyer moves from a packaging need to a shortlist of
   qualified vendors ready for the next step — whether that's contact,
   comparison, or quotation.
4. **SEO.** Most acquisition will come from organic search (Korean:
   Naver and Google). Treat SEO as a first-class concern, not an
   afterthought.

## What Packlinx is NOT

* Not a transaction platform (yet). We connect, we don't process
  payments.
* Not a B2C marketplace. Buyers are businesses, not consumers.
* Not a freelancer marketplace (we list companies, not individuals).
* Not a global platform. Korean market focus.
* Not a content site. We are a utility, not a magazine.

## Decision filter

Every decision should pass this question:

**"Does this make matching faster, more accurate, or more trustworthy
for Korean B2B buyers seeking packaging vendors?"**

If the answer is no, deprioritize. If the answer is yes, evaluate cost
vs. impact at solo-operator scale.

## Anti-patterns

* Designing for prestige instead of trust
* Adding features for "engagement" instead of vendor discovery
* Treating buyers as consumers (they optimize for risk reduction, not
  delight)
* Copying B2C marketplace patterns without translation
* Over-investing in UX polish before vendor density exists
* Building anything that requires more than one human to operate

# Packlinx Vision

## Where we are going

Packlinx will become the **irreplaceable B2B discovery infrastructure**
of Korea's packaging industry.

## What "irreplaceable" looks like

The state where vendors treat exposure on Packlinx as a *necessity, not
an option*. When this state is reached, the following signals will be
observable:

* Vendors proactively audit and request corrections to their own listings
* Vendors initiate paid placement requests without prompting
* New packaging companies treat Packlinx registration as a default
  market-entry step
* Buyers default to "search on Packlinx" as the first action when
  sourcing packaging vendors

## Transition path

| Dimension    | Today                         | Next phase                          | End state                                     |
| ------------ | ----------------------------- | ----------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------- |
| Data         | We aggregate public info      | Vendors self-register and self-edit | Vendors pay to control their listing          |
| Position     | Information aggregator        | Market-standard directory           | Industry infrastructure                       |
| Relationship | We find vendors               | Vendors find us                     | Vendors depend on us                          |
| Value        | Solving information asymmetry | Trust-based matching                | Standard advertising channel for the industry |

## What we are NOT pursuing

* Remaining a passive information directory
* Becoming a transaction platform (pricing and payments are not our domain)
* Global expansion (until Korean market leadership is secured)
* B2C extension (our users are businesses, not consumers)

## The single measure of success

The vision is realized when this statement is true:

**"Every packaging-sourcing decision and every packaging-sales exposure
attempt in Korea passes through Packlinx."**

## Strategic implications for every agent

When making any decision, ask:

1. Does this move us closer to vendor self-initiated participation?
2. Does this strengthen or weaken our position as the *default* search
   surface?
3. Would a competitor with more capital be able to replicate this in
   six months? If yes, what makes it harder by the time they try?

If your work does not affect at least one of these three, deprioritize.
